## How It Works (Universal)

**Headline:** From goals to results — four steps.

**Step 1 — Athlete assessment**
A comprehensive onboarding collects everything QFit needs to build a truly individual plan: physiological profile, training history, personal goals, and health background. Nine data categories. Seventeen questions. One complete picture of the athlete.

**Step 2 — Goals & time commitment**
Primary and secondary athletic goals are defined — from strength and hypertrophy to power, endurance, and mobility. Combined with available training frequency and session duration, QFit knows what to optimize for and how much time it has to work with.

**Step 3 — Available equipment**
The system maps the athlete's equipment access — from a fully equipped commercial gym to a minimal home setup — and uses it to constrain and optimize exercise selection. No equipment excuses. Every environment has a plan.

**Step 4 — AI-generated plan**
QFit AI (Qi) generates a fully personalized, equipment-aware, goal-aligned workout program. Not a template. Not a copy of someone else's plan. Uniquely built for this athlete, continuously updated as they train.

## PERSONA 3: SPORTS CLUBS & SCHOOLS

### The Problem — Sports Club

**Headline:** A full squad. Individual needs. One coaching team.

**Intro:** A sports club has no individual athletes — it has a population. Every player needs individual conditioning. Every coach needs to see the whole picture. Managing both manually is impossible.

**Card 1 — Individual conditioning at squad scale**
30 players, 200 students, one squad. Creating individual conditioning plans for every athlete manually is not feasible. One generic program for all means suboptimal preparation for each.

**Card 2 — Injury risk invisible until too late**
Overuse accumulates across training cycles. By the time an athlete reports pain, weeks of avoidable damage have built up. No system is watching the cumulative load across the whole squad.

**Card 3 — No comparable population baseline**
Without standardized testing across the whole squad, it's impossible to compare athletes, track cohort fitness, or identify who needs attention most.

**Card 4 — Data siloed between departments**
Conditioning coaches, trainers, and medical staff work with different data. A shared intelligence layer that everyone can read doesn't exist.

## PERSONA 4: EQUIPMENT MANUFACTURERS

### The Problem — Equipment Manufacturer

**Headline:** Outstanding hardware. But the machine doesn't think.

**Intro:** The market is moving toward platforms, not products. Hardware alone no longer differentiates. The gyms that will win are the ones with intelligent ecosystems — and the equipment inside them needs to be part of that intelligence.

**Card 1 — Hardware alone no longer differentiates**
Competitors build comparable equipment. The next battleground is data, intelligence, and ecosystem integration. Without a software layer, hardware is just steel.

**Card 2 — No training intelligence in the device**
Your equipment captures movement — but doesn't know whether that movement is serving the athlete's goal, building toward injury, or wasting effort. The insight ends at the sensor.

**Card 3 — No data flowing back to product development**
Without real usage data, product decisions are based on assumption. Which machines are used most? In which configurations? What load patterns are most common? Unknown.

**Card 4 — Gyms want integrated solutions**
Gym buyers increasingly evaluate systems, not devices. Equipment that doesn't integrate with training management software is a liability in the sales conversation.
