Section headline
The gym industry has a fundamental problem.
Intro
Members arrive with individual goals, individual bodies, and individual histories. The tools available to serve them — group classes, generic programs, over-stretched trainers — were never designed with that diversity in mind.
Card 1 — Generic training
Video libraries and group classes don't adapt to physiology, injury history, or individual member goals. One program for everyone means an optimal program for no one.
Card 2 — Trainers don't scale
Personalized coaching is expensive — and even the best trainer can only manage so many clients at once. The gap between what members need and what gyms can deliver keeps growing.
Card 3 — High early churn
Members disengage in the first 90–120 days when results, guidance, and meaningful communication are absent. That window is where retention is won or lost.
Card 4 — No operational visibility
Gym operators have limited data on member progress, equipment utilization, or training outcomes. Decisions get made on instinct rather than evidence.

Section headline
Workout planning is no longer an art. It's a science.
Intro
Traditional training tracks weight, reps, and sets. QFit goes deeper — calculating the biomechanical and physiological metrics that determine whether a session is truly advancing the athlete's goals. Every number has a purpose. Every purpose has a target.
MEE — Mechanical Energy
The total physical work performed in a session, measured in kcal. Unlike tonnage, MEE accounts for movement distance and body geometry — making completely different workout plans directly comparable on a single number.
F×TuT — Workout Intensity
Force multiplied by Time under Tension. The primary physiological driver of muscle hypertrophy and strength adaptation. QFit optimizes this metric by musculature across the entire program, not just per exercise.
WTM — Wear & Tear Metric
The cumulative mechanical load on any joint or musculature over time, adjusted for recovery. Coaches are alerted when programmed loads approach established thresholds — catching overuse injuries before they happen.
